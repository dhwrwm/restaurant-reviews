# Architecture Decision Records

An ADR records a significant technical decision at the time it was made —
including the alternatives that were rejected and why — so a later reader
doesn't have to reverse-engineer the reasoning from the code.

## Status

- **Accepted** — implemented; ADR-0001 through ADR-0006.
- **Proposed** — a direction the [Roadmap](/roadmap) commits to, not yet
  implemented; ADR-0007 through ADR-0009. These describe *why*, in advance,
  so the decision doesn't get re-litigated from scratch when the phase
  starts — but nothing on a Proposed ADR exists in the codebase yet.

## Template

Every ADR follows the same structure:

1. **Context** — the situation that required a decision.
2. **Problem** — the specific question being answered.
3. **Decision** — what was chosen.
4. **Alternatives Considered** — what else was evaluated, and why it lost.
5. **Trade-offs** — what was given up.
6. **Consequences** — what this decision constrains or enables going
   forward.
7. **References** — related pages, files, or external docs.
